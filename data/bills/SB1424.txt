Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Arizona:
Section1.Heading change
The article heading of title 41, chapter 6, article 7.2,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is changed from "LICENSING ELIGIBILITY" to
"LICENSING ELIGIBILITY AND MORATORIUM".
Sec.2.Title 41, chapter 6, article 7.2,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is amended by adding section 41-1080.01, to read:
START_STATUTE41-1080.01.Home-based business; licensing moratorium during a certain
level of unemployment; definitions
A.if the
seasonally adjusted unemployment rate, as published by the office of employment
and population statistics, reaches at least ten percent for this state, an
agency may not require a homebased business to have a valid
license.once the seasonally adjusted unemployment rate, as
published by the office of employment and population statistics, reaches less
than six percent for this state, an agency may resume requiring a homebased
business to have a valid license as prescribed by law.
B.For the
purposes of this section:
1."Agency"
means any agency, department, board or commission of this state or of any
political subdivision of this state that issues a license for the purpose of
operating a business in this state or to an individual who provides a service
to any person. 
2."License" means any permit,
certificate, approval, registration, charter or similar form of authorization
that is required by law and that is issued by any agency for the purpose of
operating a business in this state or to an individual who provides a service
to any person and the license is required to perform that service.END STATUTE
