Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Arizona:
Section1.Title 41, chapter 10, article 1,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is amended by adding section 41-1509, to read:
START_STATUTE41-1509.Tax credit; patent and trademark applications; rules;
annual report
A.The
authority shall receive and evaluate applications and certify taxpayers who
qualify for income tax credits pursuant to section 43-1075 for the costs of
filing an application for a patent or trademark with the United States patent
and trademark office.
B.The
authority shall provide application forms for the tax credit that include:
1.The
taxpayer's name, address, taxpayer identification number, telephone number and
e-mail address.
2.Information
that confirms the taxpayer's application for a patent or trademark and the
costs the taxpayer incurred in applying for the patent or trademark.
3.any other
information required by the authority.
C.the
authority shall process and evaluate each application and may request
additional information needed to verify or correct the information contained in
the application. Within thirty days after receiving a complete and correct
application, the authority shall either:
1.Issue to
the applicant a certificate of qualification for the income tax
credit.To claim the credit, the applicant must file the certificate
with the applicant's income tax return.
2.Notify
the applicant of denial of the application with specific reasons for the
denial.
D.The
authority may not approve tax credits exceeding:
1.Three
thousand dollars for any taxpayer as a credit relating to one or more
applications for patents during the calendar year.
2.Three
hundred dollars for any taxpayer as a credit relating to one or more
applications for trademarks during the calendar year.
3.Aggregate
totals of:
(a)Ten
million dollars in any calendar year for credits relating to applications for
patents.
(b)One
million dollars in any calendar year for credits relating to applications for
trademarks.
E.Income
tax credits shall be approved on a first-come, first-served basis according to
the date the application is filed with the authority. An approved amount
applies against the dollar limit for the year in which the authority received
the complete and correct application. If, at the end of the calendar year, an
unused balance occurs under the dollar limits prescribed in subsection D of
this section, the unused balance rolls over to add to the credit capacity in
the following calendar year.
F.the
authority, with the cooperation of the department of revenue, shall adopt
rules, prescribe procedures and publish forms and instructions as necessary to
accomplish the purposes of this section.
g.On or
before March 31 of each year, the authority shall:
1.Issue a
public report of:
(a)The
dollar amount of the credits approved under this section in the preceding
calendar year.
(b)The
dollar amount of credits denied under this section in the preceding calendar
year due to the dollar limits prescribed in subsection D of this section. 
(c)Whether
the credits under this section provide an actual incentive for taxpayers to
apply for patents and trademarks.
(d)Other
information the authority considers to be useful and relevant to the policies
underlying the tax credits.
2.Provide
copies of the report required in paragraph 1 of this subsection to the
governor, the secretary of state, the president of the senate and the speaker
of the house of representatives and post the report on the authority's website.
END_STATUTE

Sec.3.Title 43, chapter 10, article 5,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is amended by adding section 43-1075, to read:
START_STATUTE43-1075.Credit for costs of applying for patents and trademarks
A.For
taxable years beginning from and after December 31, 2016, a credit is allowed
against the taxes imposed by this title for costs incurred by the taxpayer in
filing an application for a patent or trademark with the United States patent
and trademark office. The amount of the credit is the amount approved by the
Arizona commerce authority pursuant to section 411509. To claim the
credit, the taxpayer must include with the taxpayer's income tax return the
certificate of qualification issued to the taxpayer by the Arizona commerce
authority.
b.If the
amount of the tax credit exceeds the income taxes otherwise due on the
claimant's income, or if there are no state income taxes due on the claimant's
income, the amount of the claim not used as an offset against the income taxes
may be carried forward as a credit against subsequent years' income tax
liability for a period not to exceed five consecutive taxable years. END_STATUTE
Sec.4.Purpose
Pursuant to section 43-223, Arizona Revised Statutes, the
purpose of section 43-1075, Arizona Revised Statutes, as added by this act, is
to encourage innovation and enterprise in this state by granting relief from
the cost of filing for patents and trademarks.
