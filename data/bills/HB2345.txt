Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Arizona:
Section1.Section 39-121.02, Arizona Revised
Statutes, is amended to read:
START_STATUTE39-121.02.Action on denial of access; costs and attorney fees;
damages
A.Any person who has requested to examine or copy
public records pursuant to this article,
and who has been denied access to or the right to copy such those
records,
may appeal the denial through a special action in the superior court, pursuant
to the rules of procedure for special actions against the officer or public
body.
B.The court may award attorney fees and other legal
costs that are reasonably incurred in any action under this article if the
person seeking public records has substantially prevailed. The court may not award attorney fees to an officer or
public body in any action under this article. Nothing in this This subsection shall does
not limit the rights of any party that is not an officer or public body to
recover attorney fees, expenses and double damages pursuant to section 12349.
C.Any person who is wrongfully denied access to
public records pursuant to this article has a cause of action against the
officer or public body for any damages resulting from the denial. END_STATUTE
