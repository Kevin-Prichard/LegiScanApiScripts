Sec.2.Title 43, chapter 10, article 3,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is amended by adding section 43-1024, to read:
START_STATUTE43-1024.Subtraction for small business investor income;
definitions
A.Except as
provided in subsection C of this section, in computing Arizona taxable income a
subtraction is allowed for fifty percent of business income received during the
taxable year by a taxpayer who owns or has an ownership investment interest in
a qualifying small business.
B.The
amount of the subtraction may not exceed one hundred twentyfive thousand
dollars of business income received by each taxpayer during the taxable year
from the qualifying small business. In the case of a married couple filing a
joint return, each spouse may include business income received individually by
the spouse from an ownership interest in the same business. The total
subtraction on a joint return may not exceed two hundred fifty thousand
dollars.
C.Business
income from a qualifying small business is eligible for subtraction under this
section only if received during the first sixty months following its
organization as a qualifying small business and regardless of any subsequent
reorganization affecting its business structure.
D.For the
purposes of this section:
1."Business
income" means income, including gain or loss, arising from transactions,
activities and sources in the regular course of a trade or business. Business
income includes Income, gain or loss from:
(a)Real
property, tangible personal property and intangible property if the
acquisition, rental, management and disposition of the property constitute
integral parts of the regular course of a trade or business operation.
(b)A
partial or complete liquidation of a business, including gain or loss from the
sale or other disposition of goodwill.
2."Qualifying
small business" means:
(a)A sole proprietorship.
(b)A partnership that is required
to file a return under chapter 14 of this title.
(c)A limited liability partnership
that has filed a statement of qualification under section 29-1101.
(d)A limited liability company
formed under title 29, chapter 4.
(e)An S corporation, as defined in
section 1361 of the internal revenue code.
(f)Any other form of business where
a distributive share of business income is subject to tax under this chapter
only in the individual capacity of its owners. END_STATUTE
Sec.3.Retroactivity
A.This act applies retroactively to taxable years
beginning from and after December 31, 2015.
B.Notwithstanding section 43-1024, subsection C,
Arizona Revised Statutes, this act applies retroactively with respect to
business income received from a qualifying small business that was organized
before December 31, 2015 as follows:
1.If a qualifying small business was organized from
and after December 31, 2014 through December 31, 2015, business income from the
business is eligible for subtraction under section 43-1024, Arizona Revised
Statutes, only if received from and after December 31, 2015 through December
31, 2019.
2.If a qualifying small business was organized from
and after December 31, 2013 through December 31, 2014, business income from the
business is eligible for subtraction under section 43-1024, Arizona Revised
Statutes, only if received from and after December 31, 2015 through December
31, 2018.
3.If a qualifying small business was organized from
and after December 31, 2012 through December 31, 2013, business income from the
business is eligible for subtraction under section 43-1024, Arizona Revised
Statutes, only if received from and after December 31, 2015 through December
31, 2017.
4.If a qualifying small business was organized from
and after December 31, 2011 through December 31, 2012, business income from the
business is eligible for subtraction under section 43-1024, Arizona Revised
Statutes, only if received from and after December 31, 2015 through December
31, 2016.
