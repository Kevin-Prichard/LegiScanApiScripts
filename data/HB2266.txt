Be it enacted by the Legislature of the State of Arizona:
Section1.Title 32, chapter 18, article 2,
Arizona Revised Statutes, is amended by adding section 32-1932, to read:
START_STATUTE32-1932.Durable medical equipment suppliers; requirements;
definition
A.A
resident or nonresident durable medical equipment supplier that has a permit to
do business in this state pursuant to this article shall submit to the board a
notarized affidavit attesting that the durable medical equipment supplier:
1.Has
at least one accredited physical facility that is staffed during reasonable
business hours per accreditation guidelines and that is located in this state
or is within one hundred miles of any resident medicare beneficiary in this
state who is being served by the durable medical equipment supplier.
2.Has
sufficient inventory and staff to service or repair products.
3.Is
accredited by an accrediting organization that is recognized and accepted by
the centers for medicare and medicaid services.
B.For the
purposes of this section, "durable medical equipment supplier":
1.Means
a person or entity that in the current calendar year either:
(a)Bills or plans to bill
the medicare program pursuant to title XVIII of the social security act and the
regulations adopted pursuant to that title for services or products listed in
the centers for medicare and medicaid services durable medical equipment,
prosthetics, orthotics and supplies competitive bid product categories or
complex rehabilitation technology in this state.
(b)Intends to bid for services
or products listed in the centers for medicare and medicaid services durable
medical equipment, prosthetics, orthotics and supplies competitive bid product
categories or complex rehabilitation technology in this state.
2.Does
not include any of the following:
(a)A person or entity
that supplies or provides insulin infusion pumps and related supplies or
services.
(b)A person or entity
that supplies or provides products that are part of medicare's national
mail-order competition for diabetic testing supplies.
(c)A pharmacy located in
this state that has a current pharmacy accreditation exemption that is accepted
and recognized by the national supplier clearinghouse and that enables the
pharmacy to be enrolled in medicare to supply durable medical equipment without
having the accreditation.
(d)A practitioner
described in 42 United States Code section 1395u(b)(18)(C) or a physician licensed
pursuant to chapter 13 or 17 of this title, if the practitioner or physician is
supplying or providing durable medical equipment to the practitioner's or
physician's own patients as part of the practitioner's or physician's own
services.
(e)A
person or entity that supplies or provides directly to a practitioner described
in 42 United States Code section 1395u(b)(18)(C)
or a physician licensed pursuant to chapter 13 or 17 of this title devices that
require a prescription for dispensing to patients as part of the practitioner's
or physician's own services, whether the devices are mailed to the practitioner
or physician for fitting or are mailed directly to patients.END_STATUTE
